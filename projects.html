Longboard Building

During my freshman year of college I started to longboard with my friend Nick. That summer we decided to try and make our own boards. We went through many designs and types of presses and ended up with a hybrid of many of the ideas we saw online and elsewhere. I honestly think our way of making boards is one of the better/cheaper ways for amateur board makers, especially those who may only want to make one or two of each board. If you're planning to make more than that, I'd recommend making a more robust press. We've since stopped making boards so I don't have as many pictures as I'd like of the process. I'll try to make models whereever I can to help clear things up.

Three Body Simulation

One year in college I was taking a class on orbital mechanics. We were reviewing the N-body problem and I thought it might be fun to simulate the three-body probloem. Almost all solutions to this have to be solved numerically, only a few special cases have algebraic solutions. I didn't have too much experience with any numerical solving methods at the time so I came up with something that made sense to me. It ended up basically being the Euler Method. If I were to redo I would use a different numerical method or even one of MATLABs ODE solvers to get something a little more accurate.
